Your skincare routine

Routine Step By Step Guide

A skincare routine typically consists of several steps that aim to cleanse, exfoliate, moisturize, and protect the skin. Here are the general steps you can follow for a basic skincare routine:

Normal Skin:

  1. Cleanse your face with a gentle cleanser to remove dirt and impurities.
  2. Apply a toner to balance the skin's pH levels and prepare it for further products.
  3. Use a lightweight moisturizer to keep your skin hydrated.
  4. Apply a broad-spectrum sunscreen with SPF 30 or higher before going out in the sun.
  5. Incorporate a serum or treatment for specific skincare concerns.

Oily Skin:

  1. Cleanse your face with a gentle or oil-free foaming cleanser to remove excess oil.
  2. Apply an alcohol-free toner to control oil production and minimize pores.
  3. Use a lightweight, oil-free moisturizer to hydrate without adding extra oil.
  4. Apply a non-comedogenic sunscreen with SPF 30 or higher to protect your skin.
  5. Apply an alcohol-free toner to control oil production and minimize pores.
  6. OptionL: Consider using a clay mask once a week to absorb excess oil.

Dry Skin:

  1. Cleanse your face with a hydrating cleanser that won't strip away natural oils.
  2. Apply a hydrating toner to replenish moisture and soothe the skin.
  3. Use a rich, emollient moisturizer to nourish and hydrate your skin.
  4. Apply a moisturizing sunscreen with SPF 30 or higher to protect your skin.
  5. Consider using a facial oil for extra hydration and improved skin texture.
  6. (Weekly): Use a hydrating mask or overnight mask to provide a moisture boost.

Combination Skin:

  1. Cleanse your face with a mild cleanser to remove impurities without drying out the skin.
  2. Incorporate a gentle exfoliant to remove dead skin cells and unclog pores (2-3 times a week).
  3. Apply a balancing toner to restore the skin's pH balance.
  4. Use a lightweight moisturizer, focusing on dry areas and avoiding oily areas.
  5. Apply a broad-spectrum sunscreen with SPF 30 or higher to protect your skin.
  6. Optional Step: Consider using different products for different areas of your face to address specific concerns.
